## Introduction

Atmospheric Water Generators or AWG's are a proven technology that extracts moisture from the Air to provide a supply of clean safe drinking water. There are several techniques for extracting water using chemical desiccants and passive heat exchangers. However currently most of these techniques are not particularly efficient and are difficult to scale.

The most reliable and high yielding technique is electro-mechanical extraction. Essentially machines using this technique are very similar to the dehumidifiers and air conditioning used for domestic and commercial buildings. The Electro-Mechanical “AWG” is a specialised version of a dehumidifier optimised to extract the maximum amount of water from the moisture in the air. The components used are all “food grade” so that the water is not contaminated and most of them include sophisticated filtration systems and UV treatment to produce safe clean contaminant-free water fit for drinking.

In the right environmental conditions, the machines can extract up to 75 % of the available moisture from the air passing through it. These types of machines have been in use for many years for small scale water production for off-grid hotels and small drinking water dispensers.

For any of the technologies to be deployed and produce water then the yield will depend on the environmental conditions. Most important of course is the amount of water in the atmosphere at the location where the water is required. For electromechanical systems to work it is necessary to condense the moisture in the air. This is achieved by cooling the air passed through the AWG down to the Dew point. At the dew point, the water will condense onto a coil and can then be collected and filtered and stored ready for consumption. The dew point will depend on the relative humidity and the ambient temperature. If the ambient temperature is too low then the dew point will be close to the freezing point and this can cause problems with the condenser freezing and low water yield. 

Because of the variations in ambient temperature and humidity, planning and understanding the characteristics of that location is key to making AWG deployments reliable and scalable.

Okeanos can modify the machines to perform better at low temperatures but in general, the machines work more efficiently in higher temperature high humidity climates. The location information is key to the effective planning and management of a water production plant.

## Okeanos location Planning tool

The environment can vary greatly depending on the location’s characteristics. Even in locations 5 miles apart there can be big differences in temperature and humidity. Altitude and air pressure also play a part. Closeness to bodies of standing water or lakes and proximity to the coast can affect temperature and humidity. Prevailing wind can make a difference as can proximity to vegetation and forests etc. The micro-climate that the AWG is located in can make a big difference to its effectiveness in producing water

Using historical weather data it is possible to calculate a guide for the likely yield of water in different locations. The actual yield will of course depend on the “real” weather in the location which may vary from year to year. But it gives us a guide to the likely performance.

The Okeanos platform incorporates historic weather data to allow us to calculate the likely production yield for any location. This unique capability means that we can predict the water yield and calculate how many AWG machines are required to meet the customer’s requirements. In cases where there are low yield months, we can specify how much storage capacity is required to ensure a year-long water supply is available.

### Control

In order to manage machine or group of machines Okeanos makes use of process control protocols to gain control of the AWG machines. Using industry standard protocols we can connect the machines together in a network and manage them as a System.

All machines supplied by Okeanos include our “**smart water control system**” the control system allows us to remotely control and monitor individual machines or complete water farms. 

Our cloud-based control system continuously collects data from all of the machines via a resilient satellite and cellular IOT system.
